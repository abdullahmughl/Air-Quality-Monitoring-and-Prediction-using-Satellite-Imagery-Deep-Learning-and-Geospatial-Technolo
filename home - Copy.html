According to Pakistan Air Quality Initiative (PAQI), in Pakistan, every year nearly 60,000 people die of health problems related to air pollution.

Lahore suffers from high levels of air pollution, with the city regularly ranking at the top of IQAir AirVisual’s live pollution rankings of major global cities. However, pollution only rose to the top of the public’s consciousness in early 2017, when actionable air quality data was published for the first time in Pakistan. In the absence of publicly available government data, a network of citizen-operated sensors began to monitor fine particulate matter, also known as PM2.5, and report data in real-time. The data laid bare Lahore’s high levels of air pollution, shocking the public and becoming a media talking point.

* We are focusing on Lahore only but other cities will be added with furthur development

To solve this problem we are using satellite images combined with the state of the art Deep Learning model to predict future values and take precautionary measures before time

To get data we are using satellites from NASA and ESA. CO2 data is collected using Orbiting Carbon Observatory-2 and NO2, SO2 and CO's data is collected using Sentinel-5P.

We are using a combination of Convolutional Neural Network and LSTM's for prediction.

All the results are available on this web portal. User can browse through different tabs to see different results.
